This document presents the results of verification and validation testing for the
Audio360 system. The tests documented here correspond to those defined in the
Verification and Validation Plan \cite{VnVPlan}. Section 3 evaluates functional
requirements, Section 4 evaluates non-functional requirements, and subsequent
sections provide additional testing insights and traceability.

the VnV Plan. Tests are organized by system component. For each test, the following
information is provided: input conditions, expected output, actual output (as
observed during testing), and pass/fail status.

\subsection{Audio Filtering Tests}

\begin{enumerate}

\item{\textbf{Test-FR-3.1} FFT conversion (time-domain to frequency-domain) \\}

\textbf{Input:}
3-second audio clip at 16 kHz with sine waves at 100 Hz, 1 kHz, and 7.5 kHz.

\textbf{Expected Output:}
Frequency-domain representation displays 3 spectral peaks at 100 Hz, 1 kHz, and 7.5 kHz with no unexpected frequencies.

\textbf{Actual Output:}
Multi-tone FFT test successfully executed. Generated 3-second signal at 16 kHz containing 100 Hz, 1 kHz, and 7.5 kHz sine waves. FFT detected all 3 target frequencies: 100 Hz peak detected (within $\pm$ 2 bin tolerance), 1 kHz peak detected (within tolerance), 7.5 kHz peak detected (within tolerance). Detection success rate: 100\% (3/3 frequencies). No spurious frequencies detected. Test completed in 6 ms total. Consistent detection validated across multiple frame positions in signal.

\textbf{Result:} Pass (X)

\item{\textbf{Test-FR-3.2} Amplitude normalization \\}

\textbf{Input:}
2-second signal at 16 kHz alternating between low amplitude (0.2 × max) and high amplitude (0.8 × max) sine waves at same frequency.

\textbf{Expected Output:}
Normalized output with consistent target amplitude (max 1.0) for both sections. Frequency unchanged.

\textbf{Actual Output:}
Normalization scaled high amplitude section to 1.0 (100\% of target), low amplitude section to 0.25 (proportional scaling maintained). Pre-normalization ratio preserved post-normalization. Three test cases passed: AlternatingAmplitudeNormalization, SilentSignalNormalization, AlreadyNormalizedSignal. All completed in 1 ms total.

\textbf{Result:} Pass (X)

\item{\textbf{Test-FR-3.3} Spectral leakage reduction (windowing) \\}

\textbf{Input:}
1-second sine wave at 16 kHz with incomplete cycle. Processed with and without windowing function.

\textbf{Expected Output:}
Windowed output has reduced spectral leakage (sharper peak, lower side-lobes) compared to non-windowed.

\textbf{Actual Output:}
Hann window reduced side-lobe amplitude by 21.3\% compared to unwindowed signal (reduction ratio = 0.787). Main lobe peak frequency preserved within 1 bin tolerance. Integer cycle test showed leakage ratio $<$ 10\% of main lobe. Three test cases passed: HannWindowReducesSpectralLeakage, MainLobePreserved, IntegerCyclesMinimalLeakage. All completed in 1 ms total.

\textbf{Result:} Pass (X)

\item{\textbf{Test-FR-3.4} Hardware-accelerated processing \\}

\textbf{Input:}
10-second signal at 16 kHz processed on: (1) microcontroller with hardware acceleration, (2) local machine without acceleration.

\textbf{Expected Output:}
Both produce equivalent spectrograms ($\leq$ 0.1\% amplitude error). Hardware-accelerated runs $\geq$ 50\% faster.

\textbf{Actual Output:}

\textbf{Result:}

\item{\textbf{Test-FR-3.5} Anomaly detection (clipping, lost signal, silence) \\}

\textbf{Input:}
Three clips: (1) amplitude exceeds 1.0, (2) signal replaced by zeros halfway, (3) 2 seconds of silence.

\textbf{Expected Output:}
Correct anomaly flags: (1) clipping, (2) lost signal, (3) silence.

\textbf{Actual Output:}
Five test cases passed: (1) ClippingDetection flagged signal with amplitude = 1.5 (100\% detection rate), (2) LostSignalDetection flagged mid-signal zero transition (100\% detection), (3) SilenceDetection flagged extended zero amplitude (100\% detection), (4) NormalSignalNoAnomalies produced zero false positives, (5) VeryLowAmplitudeNotSilence correctly distinguished low amplitude from silence. Total completion time: 2 ms.

\textbf{Result:} Pass (X)

\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Frequency Analysis Tests}

\begin{enumerate}

\item{\textbf{Test-FR-5.1} Audio classification \\}

\textbf{Input:}
Frequency-domain samples of different known audio classes.

\textbf{Expected Output:}
Predicted class matches known class with $\geq$ 90\% accuracy per class.

\textbf{Actual Output:}
Proper 90\% accuracy test implemented and executed. Classifier processed 283 frames from long\_jackhammer.mp3. Results: 1 correct classification out of 283 total frames. Measured accuracy: 0.35\% (vs required 90\%). Accuracy deficit: 89.65 percentage points below requirement (0.39\% of target). Test completed in 511 ms total. Overall accuracy across tested classes: 0.35\%. \textit{Note:} Classifier demonstrates critical systematic failure requiring fundamental algorithm revision.

\textbf{Result:} Fail (X) - 0.35\% accuracy vs 90\% required

\item{\textbf{Test-FR-5.2} Direction of arrival (DOA) estimation accuracy \\}

\textbf{Input:}
Frequency-domain samples from known directions (4-mic array).

\textbf{Expected Output:}
Estimated direction within $\theta_e$ radians of known direction. MAE $<$ $\theta_e$/2.

\textbf{Actual Output:}
Proper accuracy test implemented and executed. Tested 4 known angles (0°, 45°, 90°, 135°) with microphone array data. Results: 0° estimated at 196.8° (error: 163.2°, 362\% over threshold), 45° at 317.8° (error: 87.2°, 194\% over), 90° at 328.8° (error: 121.2°, 269\% over), 135° at 36.0° (error: 99.0°, 220\% over). Mean Absolute Error (MAE): 117.6° (vs required $<$ 22.5°). Pass rate: 0\% (0/4 angles within $\theta_e$ threshold). Test completed in 31 ms. \textit{Note:} Systematic DOA algorithm failure confirmed with proper accuracy testing.

\textbf{Result:} Fail (X) - MAE 117.6° vs required $<$ 22.5°, 0\% pass rate

\item{\textbf{Test-FR-5.3} Direction of arrival (DOA) range validation \\}

\textbf{Input:}
Random frequency-domain samples (4-mic array).

\textbf{Expected Output:}
Estimated directions output in radians, within range [0, 2$\pi$].

\textbf{Actual Output:}
Three test cases passed: (1) gccPhatAngleRange verified output in [0, 2$\pi$] range for random inputs, (2) NoDoAAlgo verified default angle = 0.0 when algorithm disabled, (3) IncorrectMicDataSize verified input validation rejects mismatched microphone buffer sizes with logic\_error exception.

\textbf{Result:} Pass

\item{\textbf{Test-FR-5.4} Low confidence notification \\}

\textbf{Input:}
Random frequency-domain samples (ambiguous) and known samples (control).

\textbf{Expected Output:}
Ambiguous samples flagged as low confidence. Known samples classified with high confidence.

\textbf{Actual Output:}
Four test cases: (1) AmbiguousSampleDetection: random noise produced 1 unique label from 5 runs (20\% consistency - expected higher variance), (2) KnownSampleHighConfidence: FAILED - jackhammer audio produced 2 different labels ("unknown" and "siren") from 5 segments (40\% consistency, expected 100\%), (3) ConfidenceDifferentiation: known samples 1/3 unique (100\% consistent), random samples 2/3 unique (67\% consistent), (4) SilentInputHandling: passed without crashing. \textit{Note:} Classifier instability issue identified in test case 2.

\textbf{Result:} Partial (X)

\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Frequency Analysis Tests}

\begin{enumerate}

\item{\textbf{Test-NFR5.1} Simultaneous classification and DOA \\}

\textbf{Input:}
Frequency-domain sample with multiple audio classes from known directions (up to 3 simultaneous sources).

\textbf{Expected Output:}
Classification accuracy $\geq$ 90\%. Direction error $\leq$ 45°. Processes 3 simultaneous sources.

\textbf{Actual Output:}
Simultaneous processing completed successfully (1.55 ms/frame). However, DOA accuracy failed with systematic errors: 0° source estimated at 309.6° (error: 309.6°, 687\% over threshold), 45° source at 292.5° (error: 247.5°, 550\% over), 90° source at 303.5° (error: 213.5°, 474\% over), 135° source at 42.2° (error: 267.2°, 594\% over). All errors exceeded 45° threshold by 474-687\%. Zero angles within tolerance. Systematic DOA calibration issue identified requiring microphone array geometry or algorithm investigation.

\textbf{Result:} Fail (X)

\item{\textbf{Test-NFR5.2} Classification accuracy \\}

\textbf{Input:}
Frequency-domain samples of known audio classes (single isolated source per sample).

\textbf{Expected Output:}
Classification accuracy $\geq$ 90\% per class across all supported classes.

\textbf{Actual Output:}
Proper 90\% accuracy test (ClassificationAccuracyTest) measured 0.35\% accuracy for jackhammer classification (1/283 correct). This is 0.39\% of the required 90\% threshold, representing a 99.61\% shortfall. Multiple audio classes tested with overall accuracy of 0.35\%. Test completed in 511 ms. \textit{Note:} Critical systematic classifier failure requiring fundamental algorithm redesign.

\textbf{Result:} Fail (X)

\item{\textbf{Test-NFR5.3} DOA accuracy \\}

\textbf{Input:}
Frequency-domain samples from known directions (single source, 4-mic array).

\textbf{Expected Output:}
Direction error $\leq$ 45° across multiple positions (0°, 45°, 90°, 135°, 180°, etc.).

\textbf{Actual Output:}
Proper accuracy test (DOAAccuracyTest.KnownDirectionEstimation) measured errors for 4 angles: 0° (error: 163.2°, 362\% over threshold), 45° (error: 87.2°, 194\% over), 90° (error: 121.2°, 269\% over), 135° (error: 99.0°, 220\% over). Pass rate: 0\% (0/4 angles within 45° threshold). Mean Absolute Error: 117.6° (523\% over 22.5° requirement). Test completed in 31 ms. \textit{Note:} DOA algorithm requires systematic recalibration or replacement.

\textbf{Result:} Fail (X)

\end{enumerate}
